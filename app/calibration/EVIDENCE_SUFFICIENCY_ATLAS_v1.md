## What this document is

```text
the constitutional memory of what institutions historically accepted as sufficient
```

Not probability tables. Not Bayesian authority. Not algorithmic sufficiency.

Institutions work through **historically stabilized sufficiency exemplars**:

- this evidence type is usually sufficient (with conditions),
- this needs corroboration,
- this alone is not enough,
- contradictions reshape confidence — they do not erase evidence.

Institutions are **not epistemically symmetric**. The Atlas must preserve awareness of **different formation pathways** for success vs failure confidence — not a unified sufficiency catalogue.

| Anchor | Role | Mechanism |
|--------|------|-----------|
| **ATLAS-B8-002** | inflation contour | confidence inflates under high legibility · felt completeness |
| **ATLAS-B8-003** | closure contour | failure stabilizes under degraded interpretive intake · institutional closure before full interpretive encounter |

```text
constitutional asymmetry pair

```

**Central restraint (failure side):**

```text
failure confidence ≠ adequate interpretive encounter
```

**Distinction B8-003 protects:**

```text
evidence was interpreted and judged insufficient
≠
evidence ecology never cohered institutionally enough to become fully interpretable
```

Exemplars with `constitutional protection: required` are **protected reminders of institutional fragility** — not heuristics · not templates · not classifier intuition seeds. **Operationalization remains blocked.**

## Exemplar format

Each atlas entry documents:

1. **Case label** — short institutional name  
2. **Evidence mix** — what was present / absent  
3. **Why sufficient or insufficient** — pedagogical reasoning (not score)  
4. **Typical confidence band** — advisory only  
5. **Contradiction modulation** — how conflicts reshape contours  
6. **What would NOT be claimed** — authority ceiling  
7. **Workshop status** — draft · deliberated · signed
